§ 02

Cheapest working architecture

One EC2 instance. One model. One FastAPI. Done. No load balancers, no Kubernetes, no service mesh. You add complexity only when a paying customer asks for it.

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ Client App ──▶ HTTPS (Cloudflare / Caddy) │ │ │ │ │ ▼ │ │ ┌──────────────────────────┐ │ │ │ EC2 (g4dn.xlarge / CPU) │ │ │ │ ├─ FastAPI (auth + route) │ │ │ │ ├─ Ollama / vLLM │ │ │ │ └─ Llama 3.1 8B · 4-bit │ │ │ └──────────────────────────┘ │ │ │ │ │ ▼ │ │ S3 (logs, optional RAG docs) │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Why this stack is ruthless on cost

  • Single EC2. No ALB ($18/mo), no NAT gateway ($33/mo), no RDS, no EKS control plane ($73/mo).
  • Ollama on one box. One binary, one model file, no orchestration overhead.
  • 4-bit quantized 8B model. Fits in ~6GB VRAM or ~8GB RAM. Runs on the cheapest usable GPU, or CPU for MVP.
  • Cloudflare for HTTPS. Free cert, free DDoS, free caching. No ACM + ALB bill.
  • Auto-stop when idle. CloudWatch + Lambda shuts the box off after X minutes of no traffic.
✓ Start with this

g4dn.xlarge — 1× T4 GPU, 4 vCPU, 16GB RAM. Comfortably runs a 4-bit 8B model at decent speed. ~$0.53/hr on-demand, ~$0.16/hr spot.

✗ Do NOT start with

p4d / p5 / H100 instances — $3–$35/hr. You will burn through a pre-seed round before your first demo call. Avoid until a client specifically pays for throughput.

§ 03

Step-by-step setup

Do these in order. Each step is under 30 minutes. Total: one focused weekend.

01
Lock down the AWS account
Create account → enable MFA on root → create IAM admin user → set a Budget alert at $50 and $100. If you skip budgets, you will get a surprise bill. This takes 10 minutes and saves rent.
02
Launch one EC2 instance
Region: us-east-1. AMI: Ubuntu 22.04. Instance: g4dn.xlarge (spot pricing). Storage: 50GB gp3. Security group: ports 22 + 443 only.
$ aws ec2 run-instances --instance-type g4dn.xlarge --instance-market-options 'MarketType=spot'
03
Install Ollama + model
Ollama is the simplest serving layer. One command to install, one command to pull a quantized model.
$ curl -fsSL https://ollama.com/install.sh | sh && ollama pull llama3.1:8b-instruct-q4_K_M
04
Wrap it in a FastAPI
~40 lines of Python. Receives a prompt, checks the API key against a dict or DynamoDB, forwards to Ollama on localhost:11434, returns response, logs token count to a file or CloudWatch.
$ pip install fastapi uvicorn httpx && uvicorn app:api --host 0.0.0.0 --port 8000
05
Put Cloudflare in front
Point api.orbitumai.com at the EC2 elastic IP. Cloudflare gives free HTTPS, DDoS protection, WAF, and hides your origin IP. Costs zero.
06
Add idle auto-stop
CloudWatch alarm: if EC2 network out < 1KB for 30 min → trigger Lambda → stop instance. Client wakes it back up via a "warm up" endpoint. Saves 60–80% of compute cost in early months.
07
Ship API keys via a one-pager
Notion or a Carrd page with: endpoint URL, example curl, pricing table, "Book a call" button. That's your entire product surface for the first 5 clients.

⚠ GPU cost warning

Spot instances can be reclaimed with a 2-minute warning. For paying clients with SLAs, switch the tier to on-demand (≈ $0.53/hr ≈ $160/mo for 300 hrs running). Bake this into your pricing — still easy 3× margin at $499/mo.

✗ What NOT to do this month

Do not provision: RDS, ElastiCache, ALB, NAT Gateway, EKS, SageMaker, Bedrock Provisioned Throughput. Each one adds $30–$500/mo and solves a problem you don't have yet.

§ 06

How to price it (vs cost margin)

Rule of thumb: price ≥ 3× infra cost. You are the mechanic, the liability buffer, and the only person the CFO can call. Charge accordingly.

Tier What they get Price / mo Your cost Margin
Starter Shared instance, 1M tokens/mo, API only $499 ~$60 8×
Private Dedicated EC2, unlimited tokens, their VPC $1,499 ~$180 8×
Secure Dedicated + audit logs, SSO, BAA, SLA 99% $3,999 ~$350 11×
Enterprise Deployed in their AWS account, you operate $7,500+ ~0 (they pay) ∞
✓ The Enterprise tier is the grail

"Deployed in their AWS" means they pay the AWS bill, you charge a flat ops fee. Zero infra risk on your side. Most mid-market companies prefer this anyway — their compliance team insists on it.

Offer A

Private ChatGPT for your company

One-line pitch: "ChatGPT that never sends your data to OpenAI, hosted in your AWS, live in a week."

Buyer: IT director at a 50–500 person company worried about ChatGPT data leaks. Price: $1,499/mo.

Offer B

Secure AI for internal docs

One-line pitch: "Upload your SOPs, contracts, policies. Your team queries them. Nothing leaves your tenant."

Buyer: Ops or Legal ops at a regulated SMB (healthcare, finance, law). Price: $3,999/mo.

§ 08

Fastest path to first paying client

In the order that actually works for bootstrapped founders:

  • List 30 target companies — SMBs, 50–500 employees, in one vertical (legal, healthcare ops, accounting firms are ideal: paranoid about data, not tech-savvy enough to build it themselves).
  • Record a 3-minute Loom demo of your private LLM answering a question with "internal company data." Generic enough to reuse, specific enough to feel real.
  • Send 10 cold emails/day with the Loom link and a single line: "I build private ChatGPT deployments. 15 min this week?"
  • First call = discovery only. No pitch. Ask what they currently do with AI and what the security team says about it. Their answer is your sales script.
  • Offer a $500 one-week paid pilot instead of a free trial. Paid pilots close. Free trials ghost.
  • At the end of week: convert to monthly or walk. If they won't pay after a working pilot, they were never going to. Move on.
✓ Target: first dollar in 30 days

Not first customer. First dollar. A $500 pilot fee in your Stripe dashboard in the first month is the only validation that matters. Everything else is theater.

§ 10

What to avoid (seriously)

  • Kubernetes / EKS. $73/mo for the control plane alone, before a single pod runs. You do not need it until you have 20+ clients.
  • Multi-region. One region covers 99% of MVP clients. Multi-region is a $2k/mo problem solving a $0 need.
  • Hosting a 70B model on day one. Requires 2× A100s. Costs $3k+/mo. Nobody pays for "a bigger model" — they pay for "my data stays put."
  • Building your own admin dashboard early. Use Retool, Airtable, or a Notion page. Your time is the most expensive infra.
  • Pre-building compliance you don't need. SOC 2 costs $20k+ and 6 months. Do it when a contract specifically blocks on it, not before.
  • Free trials. They attract tire-kickers. Paid pilots filter for real buyers.
  • Buying a fancy domain before a customer. OrbitumAI on a $12 Namecheap domain closes deals. Prove the product, then splurge.
